# UIUX Design

> AI has automated the production layer of design (wireframes, mockups, layout) but amplified the need for strategic UX thinking, user research, and design systems governance.

## AI Adoption Snapshot

| Dimension | Level | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Tool maturity | **High** | Major design tools (Figma, Adobe) have shipped native AI features |
| Task automation | **Partial** | Production work automated, strategy and research untouched |
| Industry adoption | **Widespread** | 70%+ of design teams using at least one AI tool (2026) |
| Job market impact | **Shifting** | Junior production roles shrinking, senior strategic roles growing |
| Threat level | **Medium** | Augmentation story, not replacement — but the floor is rising fast |

## Overview

UIUX Design has been one of the most visibly impacted creative fields since generative AI tools went mainstream in 2023. The impact follows a clear pattern: AI excels at production tasks (generating wireframes, creating layout variations, producing responsive designs) but cannot perform the strategic, research-driven, and interpersonal work that defines senior design practice.

The field is bifurcating. Designers who primarily pushed pixels — creating mockups, making responsive variants, aligning components — are seeing their most time-consuming tasks automated by tools like Figma AI and Galileo AI. Meanwhile, designers who conduct user research, define product strategy, govern design systems, and navigate stakeholder relationships are finding their work more valued than ever.

The net effect is not fewer designers, but a different kind of designer. The role is shifting from "make this look right" to "decide what to make and why." Entry-level positions now require prompt engineering and AI output evaluation skills that didn't exist two years ago.
