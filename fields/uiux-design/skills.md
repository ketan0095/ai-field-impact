# Skills Impact — UIUX Design

## Skills Gaining Value

### High Priority — Learn These First

| Skill | Why It's More Valuable | How To Start |
|-------|----------------------|--------------|
| **Prompt-driven design** | Every major design tool now takes text input. Your prompts are your wireframes. The quality of your input directly determines the quality of AI output. | Practice daily with Galileo AI or Figma AI for one week. Compare prompt variations to understand what produces better results. |
| **AI output evaluation** | Knowing why an AI-generated screen is bad UX matters more than generating screens. Companies need people who can evaluate, not just produce. | Review AI-generated designs against Nielsen's 10 heuristics. Build the muscle for spotting accessibility issues, inconsistent patterns, and missing states. |
| **Design systems thinking** | AI generates components. Someone must govern the system they belong to — when to add, deprecate, version, and constrain. | Study Shopify Polaris, Atlassian Design System, and Material Design 3. Understand how they handle contribution, deprecation, and documentation. |

### Medium Priority — Learn These Next

| Skill | Why It's More Valuable | How To Start |
|-------|----------------------|--------------|
| **Data literacy** | AI surfaces patterns in user analytics. You need to interpret them, spot misleading correlations, and translate data into design decisions. | Take a product analytics course. Get comfortable with Mixpanel, Amplitude, or Hotjar. Understand funnel analysis and cohort behavior. |
| **Conversational UX** | AI-powered interfaces (chatbots, copilots, voice agents) are the fastest-growing design surface. Traditional screen-based design skills don't transfer directly. | Study Google's Conversation Design guidelines. Prototype a simple chatbot flow. Understand turn-taking, error recovery, and personality design. |
| **Research strategy** | As AI handles tactical analysis (clustering, tagging, summarization), the human value shifts to designing the right studies and framing insights. | Read "Just Enough Research" by Erika Hall. Practice writing research plans that test specific hypotheses rather than open exploration. |

### Emerging — Keep on Your Radar

| Skill | Why It Might Matter | Signal to Watch |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **AI ethics for design** | Designers will increasingly be asked to make judgment calls about AI features — bias, fairness, consent, transparency. | Watch for AI ethics roles appearing in design team job postings. Read "Design Justice" by Sasha Costanza-Chock. |
| **Design engineering** | The gap between design and code is shrinking. AI-generated code needs design-literate reviewers. | Monitor whether design-to-code tools reduce the need for this, or whether they actually increase it due to quality review needs. |

## Skills Losing Value

| Skill | Why It's Declining | What To Pivot To |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Pixel-perfect mockup production** | AI generates production-ready mockups from prompts or rough wireframes. Speed of production is no longer a differentiator. | Pivot to AI output evaluation and design quality assurance. |
| **Basic responsive layout** | AI auto-generates responsive variants from a single desktop design. | Pivot to complex responsive challenges (adaptive content, interaction changes across breakpoints). |
| **Simple icon and illustration** | AI generates icons and illustrations from text descriptions. Custom icon sets for standard use cases are commoditized. | Pivot to brand illustration systems and complex visual narratives that require strategic thinking. |
| **Manual wireframing** | AI produces wireframes from text descriptions faster than manual creation. | Pivot to problem framing and requirements definition — the thinking that happens before wireframes. |

## Skills That Stay The Same

| Skill | Why AI Doesn't Affect This |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **User interviewing** | Requires human empathy, rapport, and real-time judgment. AI cannot build trust with a research participant. |
| **Stakeholder management** | Navigating organizational politics, presenting work, handling feedback, and building alignment is a human coordination skill. |
| **Workshop facilitation** | Running design sprints, ideation sessions, and critique requires reading a room and adapting in real-time. |
| **Accessibility expertise** | AI catches surface-level issues but understanding WCAG deeply and designing for diverse abilities requires human expertise. |

## Recommended Learning Path

For UIUX designers adapting to AI:

1. **First** — Learn prompt-driven design with Figma AI or Galileo AI (1 week)
2. **Then** — Build AI output evaluation skills using Nielsen's heuristics (2 weeks of practice)
3. **Then** — Study a major design system (Polaris, Atlassian) and understand governance (1 month)
4. **Then** — Learn basic data literacy with a product analytics tool (2 weeks)
5. **Ongoing** — Follow conversational UX developments and experiment with AI interface design
