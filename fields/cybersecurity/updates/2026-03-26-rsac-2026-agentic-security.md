# RSAC 2026: Agentic Security Takes Center Stage

RSA Conference 2026 (San Francisco, March 2026) confirmed the cybersecurity industry's shift toward agentic AI in security operations. Major vendors launched AI agent capabilities designed to autonomously triage, investigate, and respond to security incidents with minimal human intervention.

## Key Announcements

- **CrowdStrike** expanded Falcon's agentic capabilities with autonomous threat investigation workflows that chain detection, enrichment, and response actions.
- **Check Point** launched AI-driven security agents for network and endpoint defense, emphasizing autonomous policy enforcement.
- **Saviynt** introduced AI agents for identity governance and administration, automating access reviews and compliance workflows.
- **Multiple vendors** demonstrated "agentic SOC" architectures where AI agents handle end-to-end alert processing, escalating only complex incidents to human analysts.

## Industry Context

The agentic SOC trend reflects the broader move from AI-as-assistant to AI-as-autonomous-operator in security operations. This is driven by the persistent 4.8M global talent shortage and the volume of security events that exceeds human processing capacity. Sophos published analysis in April 2026 noting where AI in the SOC works well (high-volume alert processing, pattern matching) and where it falls short (novel threats, context-dependent decisions).

## Impact Assessment

- **Tools:** New agentic features across CrowdStrike, Check Point, Saviynt, and others
- **Automation:** Tier 1 SOC analyst workflow increasingly automated end-to-end
- **Skills:** Growing demand for practitioners who can configure and govern AI agents
- **Career:** Accelerates transformation of entry-level SOC roles; creates demand for AI SOC Engineers
