# Cybersecurity

> AI is rapidly automating frontline SOC operations while deepening the global talent shortage, making human expertise in strategic security architecture and complex investigation more valuable than ever.

## Overview

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, and damage. The field spans everything from enterprise network defense and application security to threat intelligence, incident response, and compliance. With the global attack surface expanding through cloud adoption and remote work, the industry faces a persistent talent shortage of 4.8 million professionals worldwide ([Dropzone AI, 2026](https://www.dropzone.ai/)).

AI entered cybersecurity through log analysis and signature-based threat detection in the 2010s. The shift accelerated in 2020-2024 as vendors like CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, and Darktrace deployed machine learning for behavioral anomaly detection and autonomous endpoint response. By 2025-2026, the industry moved toward "agentic SOC" architectures where AI agents autonomously triage alerts, correlate events, and execute initial response playbooks with minimal human intervention ([UnderDefense, 2026](https://underdefense.com/)).

The current state is one of **deep integration, not replacement**. AI handles the volume problem — processing millions of security events per day that no human team could review — while human analysts focus on complex investigation, strategic architecture, and decisions requiring business and geopolitical context. Gartner predicts that 50% of Tier 1 SOC analyst positions will be eliminated or transformed by automation, but demand for senior security engineers, threat hunters, and AI security specialists continues to outpace supply ([Gartner, 2025](https://www.gartner.com/)). RSAC 2026 (March 2026) confirmed this trajectory with major agentic security tool launches from CrowdStrike, Check Point, and Saviynt ([RSAC 2026](https://www.rsaconference.com/)).
