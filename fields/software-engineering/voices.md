# Voices From The Field — Software Engineering

Real perspectives from practitioners. Submit yours via [issue form](../../issues/new?template=general.yml) or PR.

---

> "The profession is shifting from 'I write the code' to 'I define what should be built, orchestrate AI to build it, validate it rigorously, and own the outcome.'"
> — Staff Engineer perspective, summarized from Pragmatic Engineer survey respondents, 2026-03

---

> "I use 3 different AI tools depending on the task. Claude Code for big refactors, Copilot for quick completions, and ChatGPT when I need to think through a design. Switching between them is a skill in itself."
> — Senior Software Engineer, mid-size SaaS company, 2026-03

---

> "As a staff engineer, AI agents actually made my job harder in a good way. I spend less time writing code and more time reviewing, designing systems, and making sure we're building the right thing. The thinking work increased."
> — Staff+ Engineer, tech company, 2026-03

---

> "Entry-level is tough right now. The tasks I did as a junior — fixing bugs, writing tests, building simple features — AI handles most of that. New grads need to come in at a higher level of system thinking than my cohort did."
> — Engineering Manager, startup, 2026-02
