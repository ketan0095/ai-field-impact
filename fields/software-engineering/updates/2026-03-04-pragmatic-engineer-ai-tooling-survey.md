# Pragmatic Engineer AI Tooling Survey 2026

The Pragmatic Engineer published its annual AI tooling survey with 900+ software engineering respondents, revealing a dramatic shift in how the profession operates.

## Key Findings

- **95% of developers use AI tools weekly** — near-universal adoption across the industry
- **75% use AI for half or more of their work** — AI is no longer supplementary, it is central
- **56% do 70%+ of their engineering work with AI** — a majority of engineers rely on AI for the bulk of their output
- **55% regularly use AI agents** — the transition from copilot to agent era is well underway
- **Staff+ engineers are the heaviest agent users (63.5%)** — experience amplifies AI leverage rather than diminishing it
- **Most engineers juggle 2-4 AI tools simultaneously** — no single tool dominates all use cases

## Tool Rankings

- **Claude Code** went from zero (launched May 2025) to #1 most-used coding tool in 8 months, with 46% naming it "most loved"
- **Anthropic's Opus and Sonnet models** received more mentions for coding tasks than all other models combined
- **GitHub Copilot** retains its lead at large enterprises (56% at companies with 10K+ employees)
- **Cursor** grew 35% in 9 months
- **OpenAI Codex** achieved 60% of Cursor's usage shortly after launch — explosive early traction

## Significance

This survey confirms the shift from the "copilot" era (AI suggests completions) to the "agent" era (AI autonomously executes multi-step tasks). The data shows that AI adoption in software engineering is no longer a question of "if" but "how much" — and the answer is "most of the work" for a majority of practitioners.
